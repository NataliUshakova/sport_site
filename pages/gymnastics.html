Rhythmic gymnastics is an Olympic sport in which athletes compete in technical skill and expressiveness in the execution of complex body movements combined with the manipulation of objects to music. Items: ribbon, ball, hoop, rope, maces.

The modern program of international competitions consists of: competitions of the individual program (four-athlon, where the gymnast performs with each apparatus); competitions in group exercises (combination, where one exercise is performed with the same apparatus for all gymnasts, the second - with two different apparatus).

The performances of the gymnasts are evaluated by the judges according to the rules of the International Gymnastics Federation (FIG) in points. The following components of female athletes' performing skills are evaluated: complexity of body and object movements, composition, technical and artistic performance. Individual exercise lasts 1 minute 30 seconds, group exercise - 2 minutes 30 seconds.

Interesting fact:

The modern program of the Olympic Games includes more than 50 sports. But only one of them - rhythmic gymnastics - was presented to the world by Russia. Rhythmic gymnastics was born in 1934, when the Higher School of Artistic Movement was opened in Leningrad at the Institute of Physical Culture named after Pyotr Frantsevich Lesgaft and the main subject of special training for students was called rhythmic gymnastics.
