Snowboarding is a sport that consists in descending from snow-covered slopes and mountains on a special projectile - a snowboard.

At the Olympic Winter Games, snowboarding competitions are held in the following sports disciplines:

- parallel giant slalom (men and women) - downhill from the mountain with a detour of the flags;

- half-pipe (men and women) - acrobatic snowboarding competitions, during which athletes move inside a large half-pipe, performing various tricks and jumps;

- snowboard cross (men and women) - 4 athletes simultaneously descend along the track with various obstacles.

Snowboarding was included in the program of the XVIII Olympic Winter Games in 1998 in Nagano (Japan), where giant slalom and halfpipe were presented. Parallel giant slalom appeared in the Olympic program at the XIX Olympic Winter Games in Salt Lake City (USA) in 2002, and snowboard cross boardcross - in 2006 at the XX Olympic Winter Games in Turin (Italy) in 2006.

Interesting fact:

Two-time Olympic halfpipe champion and nine-time X-Games winner, American snowboarder Shaun White, nicknamed Flying Tomato, has a congenital heart defect. This fact did not stop him from becoming a Burton pro rider at the age of seven.
